The ObserveAgents goal

Closing the AI blind spot.

Enterprises deploying AI systems face a critical blind spot: traditional monitoring tools were never built for the unpredictable, probabilistic nature of large language models (LLMs) and generative AI. Generative AI observability closes this gap.

What is generative AI observability?

AI observability

Full visibility into AI in production

AI observability, more broadly, is the practice of gaining full visibility into how any AI or machine learning system behaves in production — including its inputs, outputs, performance metrics, and decision patterns — so teams can detect issues, explain model behavior, and maintain reliability at scale.

Generative AI observability

Understand and improve AI-driven systems

Generative AI observability is an organization's capability to monitor, understand, and proactively improve the internal behavior of AI-driven systems — from the prompts and tools agents use to the models they call and the outcomes they produce.

Traditional monitoring vs. AI observability.

Traditional monitoring tells you a server is unhealthy. AI observability tells you why an agent misbehaved — and what to do about it.

Feature Traditional Monitoring AI Observability
Approach Reactive – alerts after failure Proactive – predicts before failure
Scope Predefined metrics & thresholds Open-ended, unknown-unknown detection
Data types Metrics & logs Metrics, logs, traces, metadata, lineage
AI/LLM support Not designed for probabilistic models Built for LLM outputs, hallucinations, token usage
Root cause Identifies what broke Identifies why it broke & patterns
Adaptability Static rules Dynamic ML-driven pattern learning
Best for Infrastructure health Complex AI-driven distributed systems
